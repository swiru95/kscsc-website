The KISS principle is a design heuristic that advises designers and developers to strive for simplicity in their work. It suggests that simplicity leads to increased usability and understandability, as well as reduced complexity and cost. The principle encourages designers to eliminate unnecessary elements and focus on the essential features and functionality of a product or system. By adhering to the KISS principle, designers and developers can create solutions that are easy to use, maintain, and understand.
